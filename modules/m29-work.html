Returning to Work

FMLA, the PUMP Act, pumping rights, pregnancy discrimination, the 6-week clearance myth, and the impossible math of US parental leave. The US is the only high-income country without guaranteed paid parental leave. Your client may return to work at 2 weeks because she has no other option.

Scope of PracticeYou do not provide legal or employment advice. You know the laws that protect her and connect her with resources when her rights are violated.

Key Laws

  • FMLA: 12 weeks unpaid leave. Only applies to employers with 50+ employees, employee must have worked 12+ months. Covers ~60% of workers.
  • PUMP Act (2022): Requires employers to provide reasonable break time and a private space (not a bathroom) for pumping for up to 1 year. Covers nearly all employees.
  • Pregnancy Discrimination Act: Cannot be fired, demoted, or passed over for being pregnant or taking maternity leave.
  • State laws: Some states have paid family leave (CA, NJ, NY, WA, MA, CT, CO, OR, etc.). Know your state.

The 6-Week Lie

The 6-week postpartum "clearance" was designed for a single clinical purpose: assessing incisional healing and uterine involution. It was never intended as a "return to normal" milestone. Most people are not physically, emotionally, or psychologically ready to return to work at 6 weeks — especially after cesarean delivery. But millions have no choice.
