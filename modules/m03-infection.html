Postpartum Infection

Infection is the third leading cause of maternal death. Endometritis, wound infections, mastitis, UTI, and sepsis — recognizing early signs is the difference between oral antibiotics and the ICU.

Scope of PracticeYou do not diagnose infection. You recognize fever, wound changes, and systemic symptoms and escalate promptly.

What You'll Learn

  • Recognize signs of postpartum endometritis, wound infection, and UTI
  • Understand what fever means in the postpartum period
  • Know the early warning signs of sepsis
  • Support wound care observation without performing clinical assessment

Common Postpartum Infections

Endometritis

Infection of the uterine lining, most common after cesarean delivery. Signs: fever, uterine tenderness, foul-smelling lochia, tachycardia. Risk factors: prolonged labor, prolonged rupture of membranes, cesarean, multiple cervical exams.

Wound Infection

Cesarean incision or perineal laceration site. Signs: redness spreading beyond the incision edges, warmth, swelling, drainage (especially purulent), wound opening. Onset typically 4–7 days postpartum.

Mastitis

Breast tissue infection, usually unilateral. Signs: red, warm, firm wedge-shaped area on one breast, fever ≥101°F (38.3°C), flu-like symptoms. Often confused with engorgement initially.

UTI / Pyelonephritis

Urinary tract infection can progress to kidney infection. Signs: burning with urination, frequency, urgency, flank pain, fever. Catheterization during labor increases risk.

⚠ Sepsis Warning Signs — Call 911

  • Temperature ≥100.4°F (38°C) or <96.8°F (36°C) — hypothermia can indicate severe sepsis
  • Heart rate ≥110 at rest
  • Respiratory rate ≥22
  • Confusion, difficulty staying awake
  • Skin mottling — blotchy, discolored patches
  • Not making urine
  • "She looks sick" — trust your instinct

Your Role

What You Can Do

  • → Check temperature if she feels warm — a thermometer should be in your doula bag
  • → Look at incisions: spreading redness, drainage, or opening = provider call
  • → Ask about lochia smell: foul odor is abnormal and may indicate endometritis
  • → Any fever ≥100.4°F postpartum = call provider same day

Test Your Understanding

1. Which postpartum infection is most common after cesarean delivery?

Endometritis is the most common post-cesarean infection. Signs: fever, uterine tenderness, foul-smelling lochia.

2. In sepsis, body temperature may be:

Hypothermia (<96.8°F/36°C) can indicate severe sepsis. Don't assume "no fever = no infection."

3. Mastitis typically presents as:

Unilateral red, warm, firm wedge + fever + flu-like symptoms = mastitis. Often confused with engorgement initially.
