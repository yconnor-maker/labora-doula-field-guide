Teen Pregnancy

Adolescent consent, Title IX protections, age-gap screening, and the unique clinical and social vulnerabilities of the youngest birthing people. Teens under 15 have the highest maternal mortality rate of any age group. They deserve doula care, not judgment.

Scope of PracticeYou do not provide legal counsel on consent or mandatory reporting. You understand the unique needs of adolescent clients and provide age-appropriate support.

Key Considerations

  • Consent: Minors who are pregnant can generally consent to their own prenatal care in most states. Know your state's law.
  • Title IX: Schools cannot discriminate against pregnant students. She has the right to remain in school, access tutoring, and take medical leave.
  • Age-gap screening: If the father is significantly older, assess for coercion or abuse. Mandatory reporting laws apply.
  • Clinical risks: Teens <15 have higher rates of preeclampsia, preterm birth, anemia, and cesarean delivery.
  • Social isolation: She may be rejected by peers, family, and school. Your consistent presence matters enormously.
  • Developmental stage: Use age-appropriate language. She is still developing cognitively — concrete examples, visual aids, repetition.
