Vital Signs

Blood pressure, pulse, temperature, respiratory rate. These four numbers tell you more about what is happening inside a body than any symptom report. Learning to take them accurately — and knowing when to worry — is foundational to every module that follows.

Scope of PracticeDoulas may take vital signs if trained and equipped. You do not interpret them clinically or make diagnoses. You record, you recognize abnormal ranges, and you communicate findings to the clinical team or provider.

What You'll Learn

  • Take blood pressure, pulse, temperature, and respiratory rate accurately
  • Know normal ranges for pregnancy and postpartum
  • Recognize vital sign combinations that indicate clinical deterioration
  • Understand maternal early warning criteria

The Four Vital Signs

Blood Pressure

Measured in mmHg (systolic/diastolic). Normal: <120/80. Concerning: ≥140/90. Emergency: ≥160/110. Use an appropriately sized cuff — too small gives falsely high readings. Take on bare skin, arm at heart level, after 5 minutes rest.

Pulse (Heart Rate)

Normal resting: 60–100 bpm. Pregnancy increases baseline by 10–20 bpm. Postpartum tachycardia (≥110) is a maternal early warning sign — can indicate hemorrhage, infection, PE, or anxiety. Bradycardia (≤50) is also concerning.

Temperature

Normal: 97.8–99.1°F (36.5–37.3°C). Fever in postpartum: ≥100.4°F (38°C). Any postpartum fever should be reported. Low temperature (<96.8°F/36°C) can indicate sepsis.

Respiratory Rate

Normal: 12–20 breaths per minute. Count for 30 seconds, multiply by 2. Tachypnea (≥24) can indicate PE, sepsis, heart failure, or anxiety. Often the first vital sign to change in deterioration.

⚠ Maternal Early Warning Criteria

  • Heart rate ≥110 or ≤50
  • Respiratory rate ≥30 or ≤10
  • BP systolic ≥160 or ≤90
  • BP diastolic ≥100
  • Oxygen saturation <95%
  • Any single abnormal vital sign warrants reassessment and provider notification

Test Your Understanding

1. What is the most commonly the FIRST vital sign to change when a patient is deteriorating?

Respiratory rate is often the earliest indicator of deterioration, changing before BP or pulse in many clinical scenarios.

2. Postpartum fever is defined as temperature ≥:

≥100.4°F (38°C) postpartum = fever. Report to provider same day.
