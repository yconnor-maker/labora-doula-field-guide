Housing & Food Insecurity

Shelter navigation, WIC, SNAP, emergency food access, and the cascading health effects of instability on maternal and infant outcomes. Housing is healthcare.

Scope of PracticeYou do not provide housing or financial services. You assess social needs, connect to resources, and understand how instability affects health outcomes.

The Connection

Housing instability during pregnancy is associated with: preterm birth, low birth weight, inadequate prenatal care, higher rates of PMADs, and substance use. Food insecurity affects 1 in 4 pregnant people enrolled in WIC. These are not social problems separate from health — they ARE health problems.

Resources You Should Know

  • WIC: Supplemental nutrition program for pregnant/postpartum/breastfeeding individuals and children under 5
  • SNAP: Food assistance (formerly food stamps) — apply at local DSS
  • 2-1-1: National helpline connecting to local services (housing, food, utilities)
  • Emergency shelters: Know your local DV shelters and family shelters
  • Diaper banks: National Diaper Bank Network — diaperbanknetwork.org
