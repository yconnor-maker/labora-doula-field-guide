Returning to Work

FMLA, the PUMP Act, pumping rights, pregnancy discrimination, the 6-week clearance myth, and the impossible math of US parental leave. The United States is the ONLY high-income country without guaranteed paid parental leave. Your client may return to work at 2 weeks — not because she wants to, but because she has no other option.

Scope of PracticeYou do not provide legal or employment advice. You know the laws that protect her, recognize when her rights are being violated, and connect her with resources.

Key Laws You Must Know

  • FMLA (1993): 12 weeks unpaid, job-protected leave. Only applies to employers with 50+ employees within 75 miles. Employee must have worked 12+ months and 1,250+ hours. Covers ~60% of workers. The rest: nothing.
  • PUMP Act (2022): Requires employers to provide reasonable break time and a private space (NOT a bathroom) for pumping for up to 1 year post-birth. Covers nearly all employees. She cannot be fired or retaliated against for pumping.
  • Pregnancy Discrimination Act (1978): Cannot be fired, demoted, refused hire, or passed over for promotion because of pregnancy, childbirth, or related conditions.
  • ADA: Pregnancy-related conditions (gestational diabetes, preeclampsia, depression) may qualify for reasonable accommodations under the Americans with Disabilities Act.
  • State paid leave: CA, NJ, NY, WA, MA, CT, CO, OR, RI, DC, MD, DE, MN, ME have paid family leave programs. Know your state.
From the PhysicianThe 6-week postpartum "clearance" was designed for a single clinical purpose: assessing incisional healing and uterine involution. It was never intended as a "return to normal" milestone. At 6 weeks, most people still have: pelvic floor dysfunction, sleep deprivation, ongoing lochia, active breastfeeding establishment, hormonal fluctuation, and frequently undiagnosed PMADs. But employers treat the 6-week visit as a permission slip. "Your doctor cleared you" means nothing about your readiness to return to work. It means your uterus is smaller.

Pumping at Work

PUMP Act Rights

  • → Private space that is NOT a bathroom — must have a lock and be shielded from view
  • → Reasonable break time — as frequently as needed
  • → No retaliation for pumping
  • → Access to electricity and a place to store milk
  • → If rights are violated: file complaint with DOL Wage and Hour Division

Test Your Understanding

1. FMLA provides:

FMLA provides 12 weeks of UNPAID leave — and only for employees at companies with 50+ workers who have been there 12+ months. ~40% of American workers don't qualify at all.

2. Under the PUMP Act, a pumping space must be:

The PUMP Act specifically prohibits bathrooms as pumping spaces. The space must be private, lockable, and shielded from view. This is federal law as of 2023.

3. The 6-week postpartum "clearance" was designed to assess:

The 6-week visit checks one thing: is the uterus back to normal and is the incision healing? It does NOT assess pelvic floor function, mental health, breastfeeding, sleep, or work readiness.

4. How many high-income countries besides the US lack guaranteed paid parental leave?

The United States is the ONLY high-income country in the world without guaranteed paid parental leave. This is not a talking point — it is a maternal health crisis.

5. If your client's employer requires her to pump in a bathroom, she should:

A bathroom is explicitly prohibited under the PUMP Act. She has the right to a private, lockable, non-bathroom space. Connect her with the DOL complaint process or a local legal aid organization.
