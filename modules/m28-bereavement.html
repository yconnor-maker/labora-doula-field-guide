Bereavement & Loss

Stillbirth, miscarriage, TFMR, ectopic loss, and neonatal death. Being present in the worst moment of someone's life is the hardest thing you will ever do as a doula — and possibly the most meaningful. There are no perfect words. Your presence IS the intervention.

Scope of PracticeYou are not a grief counselor. You are present. You follow her lead. You do not impose timelines, religious frameworks, or silver linings on her grief.

Types of Loss

  • Miscarriage: Loss before 20 weeks (~10–20% of known pregnancies)
  • Stillbirth: Death after 20 weeks. Approximately 1 in 175 pregnancies.
  • TFMR: Termination for medical reasons — a wanted pregnancy ended due to fetal anomaly. Grief + guilt + social silence.
  • Neonatal death: Death within the first 28 days of life.
  • Ectopic pregnancy: Implantation outside the uterus — medical emergency and loss simultaneously.

Memory-Making

Offer — don't push. Options include: holding the baby, photographs (Now I Lay Me Down to Sleep provides professional photographers), handprints/footprints, naming ceremony, blanket or outfit keepsakes, locks of hair. Many parents who initially decline memory-making regret it later. Gently offer multiple times.

What to Say (and Not Say)

Say

  • → "I'm so sorry. I'm here."
  • → "Your baby mattered."
  • → Use the baby's name if they've chosen one.
  • → "There is no right way to grieve."

⚠ Never Say

  • "Everything happens for a reason"
  • "At least you can try again"
  • "It wasn't meant to be"
  • "You're young, you'll have more"
  • "God needed another angel"
