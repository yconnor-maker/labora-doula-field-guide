Childcare Access

Subsidies, childcare deserts, safety evaluation, and the infrastructure crisis behind the curtain. Childcare costs exceed rent in most US states. Half of Americans live in childcare deserts — areas with more than 3 children per available slot. This is not a personal problem. It is a systemic failure.

Scope of PracticeYou do not evaluate childcare providers. You help clients understand the landscape and connect with subsidies and local resources.

The Crisis

  • Average childcare cost: $10,000–$17,000/year per child (more than in-state college tuition in most states)
  • 53% of Americans live in childcare deserts
  • Childcare workers earn median $13.71/hour — poverty wages for essential workers
  • Subsidies exist (CCDBG) but waitlists average 6–12 months
  • Head Start serves only 36% of eligible children due to funding limitations

Resources

  • CCDBG (Child Care Development Block Grant): Federal subsidy — apply through local DSS
  • Head Start: Free comprehensive early childhood program for income-eligible families
  • childcare.gov: Federal resource finder
  • State licensing databases: Check provider licensing status and violations
  • Military OneSource: For military families — fee assistance programs
