Intermediate Tier · Module 10

Multiples

Twin and higher-order pregnancies carry unique risks — preterm labor, preeclampsia, growth discordance, postpartum hemorrhage, and the emotional weight of caring for multiple newborns simultaneously. Understanding the clinical landscape helps you provide informed support.

Scope of PracticeYou do not assess fetal growth or determine chorionicity. You understand the higher-risk profile and provide enhanced support during pregnancy, delivery, and the intense postpartum period.

Key Risks

  • Preterm birth: ~60% of twins deliver before 37 weeks. Average twin delivery: 36 weeks.
  • Preeclampsia: 2–3× higher risk than singleton pregnancy
  • Gestational diabetes: Increased risk due to greater placental mass
  • Postpartum hemorrhage: Overdistended uterus = higher risk of atony
  • Growth discordance: One twin growing significantly less than the other — concerning for twin-to-twin transfusion syndrome (TTTS) in monochorionic twins
  • Cesarean delivery: Higher rate — especially for malpresentation of second twin

Postpartum Realities

The postpartum period with multiples is exponentially harder. Sleep deprivation is more severe, breastfeeding logistics are complex, financial strain is amplified, and the risk of PMADs is higher. Your presence as a doula during this period may be the most valuable service you provide for any client.

What You Can Do

  • → Help coordinate feeding schedules (tandem feeding support)
  • → Watch for PPH signs — higher risk due to uterine overdistension
  • → Screen for PMADs more frequently — risk is elevated
  • → Help with logistics: who holds baby A while feeding baby B
  • → Connect to Mothers of Multiples support groups
