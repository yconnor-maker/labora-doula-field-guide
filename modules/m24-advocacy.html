Advocacy & Discrimination

Medical racism, informed consent, refusal rights, and filing complaints that matter. The doula as a witness, an amplifier, and a buffer between a system that doesn't listen and a woman who deserves to be heard.

Scope of PracticeYou do not provide legal advice. You support informed consent, witness interactions, and help clients access complaint and reporting mechanisms.

Informed Consent Is a Right

Every person has the right to: receive information about proposed interventions (benefits, risks, alternatives), ask questions, accept or refuse any treatment, and change their mind at any time. Informed consent is not a form — it is an ongoing conversation. Coercion, threats, and performing procedures without consent are violations.

Your Role as Witness

  • Document what was said, by whom, and when
  • Amplify her voice: 'She said she has questions — can you explain that again?'
  • Know how to file complaints: hospital patient advocate, state medical board, OCR/HHS for discrimination
  • Support her in requesting medical records
  • Never physically intervene in clinical care — your power is in words, presence, and documentation
